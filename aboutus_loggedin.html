Gulshan Library









In recent times, it has become increasingly difficult, and ever more important, to find a good, clean and safe place where children and adults alike can come to indulge in the simple joy of opening a book and being transported to a different realm. The cost of buying new books also proves to be another hindrance, resulting in the coming generations being deprived of the wonders of the written word. Our mission is to address this deficiency, and to enrich the lives of our community by empowering its citizens’ personal, educational and professional growth. The library is dedicated to advancing literacy and fostering a habit of life-long learning in our patrons.

We aim to help make the community of Karachi a vibrant, informed, cohesive, and empowered people. In order to accomplish this, we pledge to:

  • Recognize users as our primary focus.
  • Innovate and be proactive in our approach.
  • Promote equity of access to information.
  • Encourage reading and literacy habits in both children and adults alike.
  • Aspire to be a flexible and experimental organization that encourages innovation and staff collaboration.

Simple. You have three tiers of membership from which you can choose; basic, advanced, and premium. You pay for the membership either online or in-person. Then, based on your rental allowance, you can borrow a certain number of books every month for a pre-determined period. We will come and deliver the books to your house, and when your rental period is up, we will come and collect the book from you in-person. Alternatively, you could always pick-up and drop-off the books in-person at our library.

  • Basic tier: 3 book rentals per month, plus 2 additional for good standing. Rental period of 15 days — Rs. 500.00/month.
  • Advanced tier: 5 book rentals per month, plus 3 additional for good standing. Rental period of 25 days — Rs. 1000.00/month.
  • Premium tier: 10 book rentals per month, plus 5 additional for good standing. Rental period of 35 days — Rs. 1500.00/month.

In case you're wondering about the difference between the rental periods and the membership period (1 month), your rental period for each book starts from the time it comes into your possession. So, for example, if you borrow 3 books at once in the basic tier, you will have 15 days before you'll have to return all 3 books, but if you borrow them at different times during the month, the return period will be different for each book; 15 days from when you borrowed each book.

Please note that if your rental period extends beyond your membership renewal deadline (as in the case of the premium tier), then the return date will be your membership renewal deadline, unless you renew your membership in advance. Additional days, in the case of advance renewal, will be carried over.

It is possible to request for an extension on the rental period, especially for literature books, and provided that it doesn't conflict with your membership renewal deadline, provided that you discuss this with the staff when you place your order. It will be the staff's decision on whether to grant you the extension or refuse it, however and the patron will be expected to respect it.

If you're just interested in reading literature books (fiction or non-fiction), we'd recommend going for the basic tier. If you need to borrow some books in addition to literature books, like reference books for your studies, or books for your children in addition to books for yourself, we'd recommend the advanced tier. The premium tier is really meant for either the most serious readers, or those people who may need to borrow lots of reference books for research material.

This is a status that is conferred by the library staff, at their discretion. Please note that we WILL NOT entertain requests to upgrade a patron's standing.

Simply put, there are three statuses that a customer can have; neutral standing, which all customers will start at, good standing, and bad standing. In order to get good standing, you just have to be a good patron! This means that you pay your fees on time, return books on, or even ahead, of time, and in good condition. The bad standing status is the exact opposite.

Maintaining a good standing is easy, and will result in the privilege of additional rentals and the staff being more open to rental period extensions, provided that it is discussed with them before-hand. Having a bad standing will, therefore, result in the staff being more strict, and if maintained for a long period of time, may result in your membership not being renewed.

Please note that your standing will NOT have any impact on the fees that you are charged.

While this is certainly a risk, we prefer to believe in the better side of people. However, we enforce a zero-tolerance policy for such behavior. If a patron should refuse to return our books, then we will cancel their membership immediately and pursue legal action against them. They will also be banned from our library permanently.
